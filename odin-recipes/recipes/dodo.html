Dodo

Dodo (Fried ripe plantain) served on a wooden plate.

As yellow plantain ripens, its skin turns black. In this state, it could be mistaken for being past it or gone off.

However, at this stage, it is, in many ways, at its best. Natural sugars develop when fried, caramelize into golden treats like no other.
In Nigeria, dodo is a classic snack because it goes with anything.

They can also be sliced into elegant discs which are how we prefer them.

Ingredients

  • yellow-black plantains
  • cayenne pepper(Optionally)
  • Sunflower oil
  • Salt

Directions

  1. Top and tail the plantains, then make a skin-deep incision along the spine of each one.
  2. Open the skin and remove the flesh. Slice diagonally into long discs.
  3. Put the plantain into a large bowl, add the cayenne pepper, a pinch of salt.
  4. Gently massage with your hands so that each piece is equally covered with seasoning.
  5. Be gentle because the plantain can turn to mush if too much pressure is applied.
  6. Pour the sunflower oil into a wide frying pan
  7. Put the frying pan on medium heat.
  8. While the oil heats up, prepare a surface, rack, or plate covered with kitchen paper.
  9. When the oil is hot, test it with one of the plantain discs â€“ it should sizzle.
  10. Fry the plantain in batches by placing the discs in the frying pan face down, one by one.
  11. The oil should sizzle and you will see small bubbles around each disc.
  12. Do not let the discs overlap in the pan.
  13. After 2-3 minutes, turn over each disc using fork or a spatula.
  14. The plantain should be golden brown on the outside.
  15. Remove the discs from the pan one by one when each piece is cooked, and leave to rest on kitchen paper.
  16. Serve hot.
  17. Leave the oil to cool, then store in a sealed container.

Notice

Frying on a very low heat will create oily plantain.

The thinnest parts, around the edge of each disc, will fry much more quickly, and will therefore crisp up and turn darker.

It can be used again to fry plantain and will keep for a long time.
